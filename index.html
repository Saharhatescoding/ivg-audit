IVG — Email, SMS & Retention Audit

I Vape Great · ivapegreat.com · June 2026 · GBP · full order history Sep 2022 – Jun 2026 (142,644 orders · 50,153 customers) · charts are interactive — hover for exact figures

Email drives 28% of IVG's revenue, and it is heavily reliant on campaigns (81% campaigns, 19% flows), produced by sending three campaigns a week to a single segment. The order data shows a tight, consumable reorder pattern: 41% of customers buy again, on a median 24–25 day cycle, and when they return they overwhelmingly rebuy the same format. The flows that would meet that cycle, winback and replenishment, do not exist, and ~1,300 customers a quarter are reactivating with no email helping them. Building those flows, deploying the segments that already exist, and recovering Microsoft deliverability is the program's largest near-term lever. This audit reports only what the Klaviyo account and its order data show.

Sends scaled 5×; open rate held near 40%, but the click rate didn’t

Monthly email sends vs open rate · last 12 months
Sends (left axis)Open rate (right axis)

Open rate held a healthy 36–50% all year even as monthly sends scaled from ~17K to ~393K (about 5×), so the open is not the problem; engagement quality on the open is intact. The leak is the click: click rate fell from 3.8% at low volume (May ’25) to ~1.2–1.7% on the big-volume months (Nov–Mar). Scaling sends 5× pulled in less-clicky recipients, so the click, not the open, is where over-mailing bites.

Open and click rates are recipient-weighted (Σ unique opens ÷ Σ delivered) from the campaign metrics; open rate is Apple-MPP-inflated, click rate is not, so the click trend is the cleaner engagement signal. Source: Donut Dashboard email_campaign_metrics, IVG, last 12 months.

Per-send open and click rate by domain is Klaviyo's "deliverability by domain" UI card. It cannot be reproduced cleanly via the aggregates API: the API returns unique openers over the window (one count per person regardless of how many campaigns they received), divided by total send events — which is not a per-send open rate. Recover the Microsoft sender reputation, then re-warm it. Excluding an inbox provider is a workaround, not a fix.

Per-flow diagnostics

Where the cause sits in something the report can't see (trigger, filter, delay, content), it is flagged as a verify-in-UI inference.

  • Checkout Abandoned (£55K) and Request Review (£33K) are the working flows. Keep; Checkout has good 6-message depth.
  • Basket Abandoned — broken. 189 recipients in 12 months vs 60,322 for checkout. More shoppers add to cart than reach checkout, so this should rival checkout volume. Inference: the add-to-cart trigger is mis-wired or the event isn't flowing — verify the trigger + exclusions in the UI.
  • "Recommend More" — built but off. Live, 0 sends in 12 months. Inference: disconnected trigger; the account has two "Placed Order" metrics (Shopify + WooCommerce), so it may be keyed to the wrong one — verify in the UI.
  • Two browse flows overlap. "Browse Abandonment 5%" underperforms the main one (£0.53 vs £0.83 RPR, 89% vs 96% delivery). Inference: a stale discount split — consolidate to one.
  • Welcome front-loads. Email 1 earns £2.59/recipient; emails 3–5 earn £0.30. Rework the tail with objection/education content.

Part C — What to do

Each move joins a retention fact to an email gap. In priority order.

  • Build a replenishment / winback flow at day 18–21. 41% reorder on a 24–25 day median, most formats are refill machines, ~1,300 customers/quarter reactivate unaided, and no flow fires into that window. Largest lever.
  • Reconnect the post-purchase flow and point it at the real next product. "Recommend More" is built and off. Hardware buyers reorder Air Pods (55%); Bundles buyers reorder Nic Salts. Fix the trigger and merchandise the bought-next product.
  • Acquire on, and welcome with, the higher-value entry products. SMART 5500 (£40.52 first order, £130 historical LTV) and PRO Refill (95% rebuy) start better customers than the cheapest disposable.
  • Deploy the segments that already exist and reduce blanket discounting. Production sends go to a test segment while Repeat Buyers / VIP / Win-Back sit unused. Mail the right recency segments; replace some sale blasts with replenishment reminders.
  • Recover Microsoft and add a second sign-up form before scaling sends, so a growing list isn't sent into a weak inbox from a single capture point.
